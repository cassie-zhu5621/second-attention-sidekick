# Title & Abstract (English)

## Title
**Working title:** Noticing What We Miss: Delegating the Act of Noticing to a VLM-Guided Companion Sidekick

**HRI-tuned (recommended for an HRI submission):**
- Noticing What We Miss: Delegating the Act of Noticing to a Placed Robot Companion
- (concept-name-first) Notice Delegation: A Placed Robot Companion that Watches and Reports What You Miss

Title rules for this project: **never use snap / photo / camera / capture** — those read as auto-photography
(Google Clips / Apple best-shot) and make the concept sound non-novel. Foreground the interaction concept
(**Notice Delegation**), not the technology (VLM).

## Abstract
We move through environments filled with moments, yet human attention is limited, and we constantly miss
things. To address this, we present a placed robot sidekick that proposes a new form of human–robot
collaboration: delegating the act of noticing. Set down anywhere, it understands the context, watches for
the moments worth noticing, and reports them back. The system uses a multimodal vision–language model as a
compiler of context that steers lightweight computer vision to explore whether open-ended human intent can
drive ordered visual attention. We instantiate the concept in a pan-tilt prototype and run a preliminary
study in a shared lab space, examining whether people accept and make use of a sidekick that notices on
their behalf.

## Novelty defense (vs "Google/Apple already did this")
The earlier "AutoSnap" framing read as auto-photography — fixed aesthetic/face/smile heuristics that pick a
"good" frame (Google Clips, Apple best-shot). That is NOT novel. **Notice Delegation differs on four axes:**
1. Criterion is **user-defined and rewritable in plain language**, not a fixed aesthetic model (Google Clips'
   core failure was that users couldn't tell it what they cared about).
2. It notices **relations / social moments** (who attends to / reaches for / converges on what), not "a good frame."
3. It is an **embodied, placed social agent** that orients and reports — something you delegate to, not a passive camera.
4. The contribution is the **interaction** (delegation + steering) studied as HRI, not a capture algorithm.
